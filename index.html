About

Hello World!

Oscar Lee is an undergraduate student at the University of Massachusetts Amherst, class of 2017. He is currently graduating with a Bachelor of Science degree in Computer Systems Engineering. Although a core part of his curriculum is in hardware engineering, his interests lie in software engineering as a Ruby on Rails developer at UMass Parking Services.

Within a small team at Parking Services, Oscar has the pleasure to work within the full stack: from deploying production servers, developing testing suites, and to creating client functionality. Oscar and his team meet daily with code reviews and feature designs. In practicing agile development, he works closely with Parking staff, Campus police, and UMass departments. Oscar's accomplishments, creating core code and application, will be used by students, faculty, and staff for years to come.

Academically, Oscar and his senior design team are creating Emota, a device that aids the Autistic and Socially-impaired individuals to have conversations. The device detects faces and returns the mood and emotion of the user's conversation partner/s. Oscar works with AWS (Amazon Web Services) and APIs to aggregate emotion data and returning feedback to the user. This experience brought Oscar closer to creating a realistic solution to a societal problem.

On the side, Oscar likes designing webpages for family, friends, and friends of friends. These sites are frontend/static pages with the occasional JS/JQuery. For fun, he sharpens his Photoshop skills in Photoshop battles on Reddit. If and when he is away from his computer, you may very well catch him breakdancing in Boston.
